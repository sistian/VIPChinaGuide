📍 Shanghai City Overview

Area

6,341 km²

Population

24.9 million

Name Origin

Shanghai (上海) means "Upon the Sea". Originally a small fishing village named Hudu, it grew into China's largest city and global financial hub.

Happiness Index

7.5/10 — Residents enjoy a cosmopolitan lifestyle with world-class museums, theaters, and dining.

Metro & Transport

Airport: Shanghai Pudong (PVG) — 2 terminals (S1/S2), 40 min to city by Maglev (¥50, 431 km/h) or Metro Line 2. Shanghai Hongqiao (SHA) — 2 terminals (T1/T2), 20 min to city center by Metro Line 2/10. Train Stations: Shanghai Hongqiao (high-speed rail hub, 30 min to Bund), Shanghai Railway Station (city center, near Jing'an), Shanghai South (regional trains).

Universities

Fudan, Jiao Tong, Tongji, NYU Shanghai

Natural Resources

Huangpu River, Chongming Island wetlands, Dishui Lake, Dianshan Lake.

🍜 Shanghai Food & Shopping Guide

🍽️ Must-Try Foods

  • Xiaolongbao (小笼包) — Soup dumplings from Nanxiang Mantou Dian, 140 years old
  • Shengjianbao (生煎包) — Pan-fried pork buns with crispy bottoms at Yang's Fry Dumpling
  • Hairy Crab (大闸蟹) — Seasonal delicacy from Yangcheng Lake, best in October-November
  • Congyoubing (葱油饼) — Scallion oil pancake, crispy street snack from old alleyways
  • Hongshaorou (红烧肉) — Braised pork belly in soy sauce, a Shanghainese comfort food
  • Shanghai Breakfast Four Treasures (四大金刚) — Fried dough, soy milk, rice cake, and sesame balls

🛍️ Best Shopping Spots

  • Nanjing Road (南京路) — China's busiest shopping street, from luxury to local brands
  • Tianzifang (田子坊) — Creative arts lane with indie boutiques, cafes, and craft studios
  • Xintiandi (新天地) — Upscale shopping and dining in restored Shikumen lane houses
  • AP Plaza (亚太盛汇) — Underground market at Shanghai Science & Technology Museum for bags, watches, and accessories
  • South Bund Fabric Market (南外滩轻纺市场) — Custom-tailored suits and dresses in 2-3 days

📸 Hot Photo Spots

  • The Bund at sunrise (外滩) — Colonial buildings against the Lujiazui skyline, best at 6:00 AM
  • Lujiazui ring pedestrian bridge (陆家嘴环形天桥) — Framed shot of the Oriental Pearl Tower and skyscrapers
  • Yu Garden zigzag bridge (豫园九曲桥) — Classical garden with tea house and red lanterns
  • The Shanghai Tower 118th floor (上海中心) — 546m above ground, panoramic city views
  • Wukang Road (武康路) — French Concession sycamore-lined street with historic villas

🏛️ Intangible Heritage & Craftsmen

  • Shanghai Silk Embroidery (上海刺绣) — Master Zhu Meiling's 'Random Stitch' technique creates photorealistic embroidery at the Shanghai Museum, using over 300 needle types.
  • Shikumen Architecture (石库门) — Heritage conservationist Wang Shixiang preserves the city's iconic lane houses, blending Chinese courtyard with Western row-house design.
  • Shanghai Opera (沪剧) — Performer Ma Lili continues the 200-year-old local opera form, known for its lyrical storytelling of working-class Shanghai life.

Shanghai Colonial & Modern Contrast Tour

Most Popular 1 Day

Experience the dramatic collision of 1920s colonial glamour and 21st-century futurism that defines modern Shanghai.

The Bund

A 1.5-kilometer waterfront promenade lined with 52 buildings representing every major architectural style from Gothic to Art Deco to Romanesque Revival. Built between 1890-1930 by British, French, and American banks, trading houses, and consulates, these buildings once formed the financial heart of Asia. Your guide reveals the secret histories — the HSBC building's marble mosaic ceiling depicting the world's seven continents, the Customs House's Big Ben-inspired clock tower, and the Peace Hotel where Charlie Chaplin and Noel Coward stayed.

Oriental Pearl Tower

A 468-meter TV tower built in 1994 featuring 11 spheres of varying sizes inspired by a Tang Dynasty poem about pearls falling on a jade plate. The observation deck at 263 meters offers 360-degree views spanning both historic Puxi and futuristic Pudong. Your guide points out the urban planning miracle that transformed Pudong from farmland and fishing villages into China's financial center with 4,000+ skyscrapers in just 30 years.

Yu Garden & Old Town

A 400-year-old classical garden built during the Ming Dynasty by a government official for his aging parents. The garden features rockeries made from 2,000 tons of yellow limestone from Lake Tai, the famous Exquisite Jade Rock with 72 holes that creates whistling sounds when incense smoke passes through, and the Nine-Turning Bridge designed to confuse evil spirits. Your guide navigates through the surrounding bazaar of traditional crafts, explaining how this area preserves pre-colonial Shanghai culture.

Shanghai Art & Design Discovery

Cultural 1 Day

Dive into Shanghai's thriving creative scene, from propaganda poster museums to cutting-edge design centers housed in converted industrial spaces.

M50 Art District

A former textile mill compound on Suzhou Creek that has become Shanghai's premier contemporary art cluster. Over 140 galleries and studios occupy 1930s industrial warehouses with original iron pillars and exposed brick walls. Your guide leads you through avant-garde Chinese art installations, explains the political subtexts in contemporary works, and introduces you to artists whose works sell for millions at Sotheby's and Christie's auctions.

Propaganda Poster Art Center

A hidden museum in the basement of an apartment building housing 6,000 original propaganda posters from 1949-1990. Your guide decodes the visual language of Mao-era political art — the heroic worker poses with muscles exaggerated to Soviet-realist proportions, the color symbolism of red representing revolution, and how these posters evolved from fierce anti-American campaigns to cheerful socialist-realist scenes of bountiful harvests.

Tianzifang

A labyrinth of 1930s Shikumen lane houses transformed into boutique studios, cafes, and craft workshops while retaining their residential character — locals still hang laundry between art galleries. Your guide leads you through hidden alleys to meet paper-cutting masters whose designs require 20+ hours of scissor work, seal-carving artists using 2,000-year-old techniques, and traditional calligraphers who continue family trades spanning six generations.
