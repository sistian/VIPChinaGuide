📍 Changsha City Overview

Area

11,819 km²

Population

10.0 million

Name Origin

Changsha (长沙) means "Long Sandy Shore". Capital of the Chu Kingdom in ancient times. Famous for its spicy food culture.

Happiness Index

7.5/10 — A city of night owls and spice lovers — late-night crayfish, stinky tofu, and vibrant entertainment.

Metro & Transport

Airport: Changsha Huanghua (CSX) — 2 terminals (T1/T2), 30 min to city center by Maglev (¥20) or Metro Line 6. Train Stations: Changsha South (high-speed rail hub, 20 min to Wuyi Square), Changsha Railway Station (city center, near Orange Isle), Changsha West (regional). Metro: 6 lines. Ferry: Boats to Orange Isle from the riverbank.

Universities

Central South University, Hunan University, National University of Defense Technology

Natural Resources

Xiang River, Yuelu Mountain, Orange Island, Dawei Mountain, Heimifeng National Forest, Meixi Lake.

🍜 Changsha Food & Shopping Guide

🍽️ Must-Try Foods

  • Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐) — Changsha's most famous street snack: fermented tofu deep-fried until crispy and served with chili sauce
  • Spicy Crayfish (口味虾) — Whole crayfish in a spicy, numbing broth of Sichuan pepper and chili, a Changsha summer obsession
  • Chopped Chili Fish Head (剁椒鱼头) — Massive fish head steamed under a blanket of fermented red chili, a Hunan signature
  • Rice Noodles (米粉) — Changsha's breakfast staple: rice noodles in rich bone broth with braised pork, pickled beans, and scallion
  • Sugar Oil Cake (糖油粑粑) — Glutinous rice balls fried in molasses until golden and sticky, a sweet street snack
  • Smoked Pork (湘西腊肉) — Hickory-smoked pork from western Hunan, served at every Changsha restaurant

🛍️ Best Shopping Spots

  • Wuyi Square (五一广场) — Changsha's commercial heart with department stores and underground shopping malls
  • Taiping Street (太平街) — Historic pedestrian street with snack stalls, boutiques, and traditional crafts
  • Jiefang West Road (解放西路) — Nightlife district with bars, clubs, and street food vendors
  • Huogongdian Temple (火宫殿) — Snack market inside a temple complex, famous for stinky tofu and sugar oil cakes
  • Hunan Provincial Museum Shop (湖南省博物馆) — Replicas of the 2,000-year-old Mawangdui silk paintings and lacquerware

📸 Hot Photo Spots

  • Orange Isle (橘子洲) — The massive sandbar in the Xiang River with a 32m statue of young Mao Zedong, best viewed from the riverbank at sunset
  • Yuelu Mountain (岳麓山) — Autumn maple leaves and the 1,000-year-old Yuelu Academy at the mountain's foot
  • Aiwan Pavilion (爱晚亭) — The 'Love Evening' pavilion surrounded by red maples, immortalized in Tang dynasty poetry
  • Changsha IFS (国金中心) — The 452m skyscraper with the famous KAWS sculpture on the 7th floor rooftop garden
  • Mawangdui Han Tombs (马王堆) — The 2,000-year-old tomb of Lady Xin Zhui, with perfectly preserved body and silk garments

🏛️ Intangible Heritage & Craftsmen

  • Hunan Embroidery (湘绣) — Master Liu Aiyun at the Hunan Embroidery Museum creates double-sided, almost photorealistic embroidery using the 'hairsplitting' technique, where silk threads are split into 1/64th of a hair's width.
  • Changsha Tongguan Kiln (铜官窑) — Ceramicist Liu Kunshou continues the Tang dynasty underglaze blue-and-green porcelain tradition from the ancient Tongguan kiln site on the Xiang River.
  • Hunan Flower Drum Opera (花鼓戏) — Performer Li Yulan sings the lively folk opera of Hunan, combining local dialect, acrobatics, and bamboo clapper rhythms in the distinctive Changsha style.

Changsha Yuelu Mountain & Mawangdui Tombs

Most Popular 1 Day

Hike the mountain where Chinese philosophy was debated 1,000 years ago, then marvel at a 2,100-year-old mummy so well preserved her skin remained elastic.

Yuelu Academy

One of the four most prestigious academies in imperial China, founded in 976 AD and operating continuously for 1,000 years. Your guide walks you through the academy where philosophers debated Neo-Confucianism, points out the 1,300-year-old ginkgo tree where students carved their names, and explains the academy's influence on Chinese education — its curriculum became the model for all imperial examinations until 1905.

Mawangdui Han Tombs

The 2,100-year-old tombs of a Han Dynasty noble family containing one of the world's best-preserved ancient bodies — Lady Dai Xinzhui's mummy remains so intact that her skin was still elastic, her joints movable, and her Type A blood identifiable when discovered in 1971. Your guide explains the 20 layers of silk and hemp that preserved her, the 3,000 artifacts buried with her including silk paintings depicting Daoist immortality beliefs, and the 162 wooden figurines representing her servants.

Orange Isle (Juzizhou)

A 6-kilometer sandbar in the Xiang River that has been a Changsha landmark for 1,700 years. Your guide leads you to the 32-meter Youth Mao Zedong statue at the island's head, explains the poem Mao wrote here in 1925 that launched his political career, and walks you through the orange groves that give the island its name — 2,000 trees producing fruit that was once reserved exclusively for imperial tribute.
