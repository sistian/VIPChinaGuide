📍 Xishuangbanna City Overview

Area

19,700 km²

Tropical rainforest region bordering Myanmar and Laos

Population

1.3 Million

Dai ethnic minority: ~30% of population, plus Hani and Jino

Name Origin

Xishuangbanna (西双版纳) comes from the Dai language, meaning 'Twelve Thousand Fields' — referring to the ancient Dai kingdom's twelve rice-growing regions. It is China's only tropical rainforest nature reserve and a center of Theravada Buddhism, with over 500 Dai temples scattered across the countryside.

Happiness Index

8.0/10 — A tropical paradise with warm weather year-round, vibrant ethnic culture, and abundant nature.

Metro & Transport

Airport: Xishuangbanna Gasa (JHG) — 1 terminal, 15 min to Jinghong city center by taxi (¥20). Train Stations: Xishuangbanna Railway Station (new high-speed rail, 20 min to Jinghong), connecting to Kunming (3 hours) and Dali. Getting Around: Taxis and shared vans; many tourists rent a car with driver. Border: Checkpoints for travel to Myanmar border areas; foreigners need permits for some zones.

Universities

Yunnan University Xishuangbanna Tropical Botanical Garden Research Station — world-leading tropical botany research.

Natural Resources

Tropical rainforest with 13,000 plant species, wild Asian elephants, gibbons, and peacocks. The Lancang (Mekong) River flows through the region. Average temperature: 22°C year-round.

🍜 Xishuangbanna Food & Shopping Guide

🍽️ Must-Try Foods

  • Dai Grilled Chicken (傣味烤鸡) — Whole chicken marinated in Dai herbs and grilled over charcoal, served with sweet chili sauce
  • Bamboo Rice (竹筒饭) — Glutinous rice cooked in fresh bamboo with coconut milk and banana leaves, a Dai staple
  • Pineapple Rice (菠萝饭) — Rice steamed inside a hollow pineapple with raisins, shrimp, and cashews
  • Dai Cold Noodles (傣族米线) — Rice noodles with sour tamarind dressing, peanuts, and fresh herbs, perfect for tropical heat
  • Dai Pickled Vegetables (傣味酸菜) — Fermented vegetables with chili and garlic, served with every Dai meal
  • Wild Mushroom Hotpot (野生菌火锅) — Fresh porcini, boletus, and chanterelles gathered from the rainforest, cooked in chicken broth

🛍️ Best Shopping Spots

  • Manting Park Night Market (曼听公园夜市) — Dai ethnic crafts, silver jewelry, and tropical fruit stalls
  • Jinghong Farmers Market (景洪农贸市场) — Fresh tropical fruits (mango, durian, passion fruit), dried mushrooms, and Pu'er tea
  • Dai Ethnic Village Shops (傣族村寨) — Handmade bamboo crafts, woven baskets, and hand-pressed Pu'er tea cakes
  • Xishuangbanna Tea Factory (茶厂) — Buy aged Pu'er tea from ancient tea trees, some over 800 years old
  • Tropical Rainforest Souvenir Shop (热带雨林) — Hand-carved wooden elephants, peacock feathers, and tropical honey

📸 Hot Photo Spots

  • Dai Ethnic Village (傣族园) — Traditional stilt houses, peacocks, and Buddhist pagodas in the Dai minority villages
  • Xishuangbanna Tropical Botanical Garden (植物园) — 13,000 species of tropical plants, giant water lilies, and rainforest canopy walks
  • Manting Park (曼听公园) — The former Dai royal garden with white Buddhist temples, pagodas, and peacocks
  • Wild Elephant Valley (野象谷) — Walkways through the rainforest where wild Asian elephants roam, best at dawn
  • Jinghong Night Market (景洪夜市) — Hundreds of red lanterns, street food stalls, and Dai dancing under the tropical sky

🏛️ Intangible Heritage & Craftsmen

  • Dai Slow Drum Dance (傣族慢轮制陶) — Potter Yubo in Manzhang village continues the 3,000-year-old Dai pottery tradition, using a hand-turned slow wheel to create unglazed earthenware for religious ceremonies.
  • Dai Peacock Dance (孔雀舞) — Dancer Dao Meilan performs the traditional Dai peacock dance, mimicking the movements of peacocks with graceful hand gestures and flowing silk skirts, a dance passed down through Dai priestesses.
  • Dai Paper-making (傣族造纸) — Elder Ai Lun in Mangtuan village makes paper from the bark of the 'gou' tree, a 800-year-old Dai tradition used for Buddhist scriptures and decorative umbrellas.

Xishuangbanna Tropical Rainforest & Dai Villages

Most Popular 1 Day

Enter China's only tropical rainforest, where 5,000 plant species thrive and the Dai people have built bamboo houses on stilts for 2,000 years.

Xishuangbanna Tropical Botanical Garden

China's largest tropical botanical garden at 900 hectares, containing 13,000 species of tropical plants including 100 varieties of orchids and 30 species of carnivorous plants. Your guide leads you through the rainforest canopy walkway 30 meters above ground, explains the 'strangler fig' trees that slowly suffocate host trees over 200 years, and points out the 'walking palm' that literally moves 20 centimeters per year toward sunlight by growing new roots.

Dai Ethnic Village (Ganlanba)

A traditional Dai village where families still live in bamboo stilt houses built 2 meters above ground to avoid flooding and wild animals. Your guide explains the Dai's Theravada Buddhist tradition distinct from Chinese Mahayana Buddhism, arranges a water-splashing festival demonstration where participants splash water for good luck, and introduces you to the 'peacock dance' — a 1,000-year-old court dance performed at temple festivals.

Wild Elephant Valley

A 370-hectare nature reserve that is the only place in China where wild Asian elephants still live — about 250 elephants roam freely across rainforest and grassland. Your guide arranges a treehouse observation session where you can watch elephants bathe in rivers from 20 meters above ground, explains the matriarchal elephant society where grandmothers lead herds of 30+ family members, and shares the tragic history of how China's elephant population dropped from 10,000 to 200 due to deforestation.
